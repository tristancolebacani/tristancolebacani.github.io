Hi! I'm Tristan Cole Bacani

I am a 19 year old musician and future engineer. Originally from the San Francisco Bay Area, I moved across the country to pursue my Bachelor of Engineering from Stevens Institute of Technology in Hoboken, NJ. I am majoring in Mechanical Engineering and minoring in Biomedical Engineering.

The ability to innovate impactful solutions is what drew me to enineering. I am driven by the ability to create models, prototypes, and experiments that can benefit society. My dream is to combine music and technology, as well as create equity through engineering. I strive to advocate for women in S.T.E.A.M. and share my own experience as a minority woman in tech through outreach and dialogue.

Research

This summer, I am pursuing virtual research with The Musculoskeletal Control and Dynamics Lab at Stevens Institute of Technology

"Our research focuses on understanding and improving movement mechanics used during athletic and everyday maneuvers. Specifically, we are interested in (1) uncovering strategies people use to achieve the mechanical objectives of turning maneuvers and (2) developing interactive sonification as a modality of personalized biofeedback. Sonificatied biofeedback conveys biomechanics metrics via sound. We are developing systems with which people can interactively “tune” music biofeedback to tune their movement strategies."
